Gluvelo is a personal health tracking app for people managing chronic conditions, medications, and dietary needs. This policy explains clearly what we collect, why, and what we do and don't do with it.

1. About This Policy & HIPAA

Gluvelo is not a HIPAA-covered entity. We are not a healthcare provider, health plan, or healthcare clearinghouse, and we do not conduct electronic healthcare transactions as defined under HIPAA. The federal HIPAA privacy protections that apply to your doctor or insurance company do not automatically apply to Gluvelo.

That said, we treat your health data with the same seriousness as if HIPAA applied — because we believe your health information is among the most sensitive data you can share. The protections in this policy are our voluntary commitment to you, enforceable under applicable state and federal consumer protection laws.

2. What We Collect

When you use Gluvelo, we store the following on your account:

  • Your first name and email address
  • Health profile: conditions, medications, dietary restrictions, food sensitivities, biological sex
  • Logs you create: injection records, side effect entries, food scans, weight and measurements, vial inventory, supplement tracking
  • Journal entries you write
  • App usage: streak counts, onboarding completion, notification preferences
  • Device and usage data: device type, operating system, app version, crash reports, and in-app behavior (screens visited, features used) — collected to improve app performance and reliability

We do not collect your precise location, contacts, payment information, Social Security number, government-issued ID, or any data you have not explicitly entered or that is not generated automatically by your use of the app.

3. Sensitive Health Data

Under applicable privacy laws — including the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), the Washington My Health My Data Act (MHMDA), and others — certain categories of information you log in Gluvelo are classified as sensitive personal information or consumer health data. These include:

  • Health conditions (Type 2 Diabetes, Pre-diabetes, chronic conditions)
  • Medication and treatment records (GLP-1 medications, peptides, supplements)
  • Injection logs, dosage records, and titration data
  • Side effect journals
  • Body measurements (weight, waist, hips) — considered biometric data in some jurisdictions
  • Food tolerance and reaction logs
  • UTI history, if logged

We apply heightened protections to this category of data. We do not sell your identifiable health data to any party. We do not use it for advertising. See Section 11 for how anonymized, de-identified aggregate data may be used.

4. How We Use Your Data

Your personal data is used to make the app work for you:

  • To display your protocol, logs, and progress inside the app
  • To generate your personal health record PDF
  • To send dose reminders and vial expiry notifications (only if you enable them)
  • To provide food scanner verdicts calibrated to your health profile
  • To maintain your streak and milestone history
  • To diagnose crashes and technical issues (via crash reporting)
  • To understand how features are used so we can improve the app (via behavioral analytics)

We do not use your personal or identifiable health data for advertising, and we do not sell it to third parties. De-identified aggregate data may be used for research purposes as described in Section 11.

5. Algorithmic Processing

Gluvelo's food scanner uses automated logic to generate Green, Amber, or Red verdicts based on nutritional data and your health profile. This is algorithmic decision-making that affects how information is presented to you. The verdict logic weighs factors such as sugar content, fat content, glycemic index, fiber, and your logged conditions (e.g. diabetes weighting).

These verdicts are estimates based on third-party nutritional data and your self-reported profile. They are not medical recommendations. We do not use your identifiable health data to train external AI models.

6. Data Retention

  • Active account: All data you log is retained for as long as your account is active.
  • After account deletion: Your personal data is permanently deleted from our live systems within 30 days of account deletion.
  • Backups: Residual copies in automated backup systems may persist for up to 90 days before being overwritten, after which they are fully purged.
  • De-identified aggregate data: Anonymized data derived from usage patterns may be retained indefinitely. This data cannot be linked back to you.
  • Legal holds: If we are required by law to retain certain data, we will retain only what is legally required and for no longer than the law mandates.

7. Where Your Data Is Stored

Your data is stored in Google Firebase (Firestore), a cloud database with industry-standard encryption at rest and in transit. Firebase servers are operated by Google and may be located in the United States or other countries where Google operates data centers. Only you can access your own data — Gluvelo's Firestore security rules enforce this at the server level.

App infrastructure and any web-facing services may be hosted on Vercel, which processes request metadata (such as IP addresses and request logs) as part of normal hosting operations. Vercel does not have access to your health data stored in Firebase.

8. Security & Breach Notification

We use the following measures to protect your data:

  • Encryption at rest and in transit (provided by Firebase/Google)
  • Server-side Firestore rules that restrict each user to their own data
  • Minimum 8-character password enforcement at account creation
  • Firebase Authentication for identity management
  • No storage of API keys or credentials in the app's source code

No system is perfectly secure. If we become aware of a data breach that affects your personal or health information, we will notify you via email within 72 hours of discovering the breach, and we will cooperate with applicable regulatory authorities as required by law.

9. Who We Share It With

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal health data with third parties. The services that may handle your data are:

  • Google Firebase (Firestore & Auth) — secure data storage and authentication. Firebase Privacy Policy
  • Firebase Analytics (Google) — in-app usage analytics to understand how features are used. Collects anonymized usage events (screens viewed, features tapped). No health data fields are sent. Firebase Privacy Policy
  • Firebase Crashlytics (Google) — crash reporting. Collects device info, OS version, app version, and stack traces when the app crashes. No health data is included in crash reports. Firebase Privacy Policy
  • Behavioral Analytics (e.g. Mixpanel or Amplitude) — in-app behavior tracking to understand feature usage patterns. Only anonymized events are transmitted — no names, emails, or health data fields. Mixpanel Privacy Policy / Amplitude Privacy Policy
  • Vercel — web hosting infrastructure. Processes request metadata (IP address, request logs) as part of normal hosting operations. Does not access your health data. Vercel Privacy Policy
  • Open Food Facts — when you scan a barcode, the barcode number is sent to their public API. No personal data is sent. Open Food Facts Privacy Policy
  • Expo Notifications — delivers push notifications if you enable them. No health data is transmitted. Expo Privacy Policy

We do not use advertising networks or data brokers. If new third-party services are added that handle personal data, we will update this policy and notify you in the app before that sharing begins.

10. No Sale of Personal Data to Insurers, Employers, or Data Brokers

We will never sell, share, or disclose your personal or identifiable health data — including medication logs, conditions, body measurements, or any other information that can be linked to you — to insurance companies, employers, or data brokers, under any circumstances.

De-identified aggregate data (data that has been stripped of all identifying information and cannot be linked back to any individual) may be licensed to pharmaceutical companies, compounding pharmacies, or academic researchers for lawful health research purposes, as described in Section 11.

11. De-Identified & Anonymized Data

We may create de-identified, anonymized data sets derived from aggregate usage patterns — for example, the percentage of users who log a particular side effect in a given week, or general food scanning trends across the user base. This data is processed so that it cannot reasonably be used to identify any individual user.

De-identified aggregate data may be:

  • Used internally to improve the app and its features
  • Licensed to pharmaceutical companies, compounding pharmacies, or academic research institutions for legitimate health research purposes
  • Published in aggregated, anonymized form in research or product insights

We will never attempt to re-identify anonymized data. We do not sell raw, identifiable user records under any circumstances. Any third party that receives de-identified data is contractually prohibited from attempting to re-identify it.

12. Notifications

If you enable push notifications, we use Expo's notification service to deliver dose reminders and vial expiry alerts. You can turn these off at any time in your device settings or from the Profile tab in the app.

15. Age Requirements

Gluvelo is intended for users 18 years of age and older. Given the nature of the health data tracked — including prescription and compounded medications, injection logs, and chronic conditions — the app is not appropriate for minors.

We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18. If we become aware that a user under 18 has created an account, we will promptly delete their account and all associated data. If you believe a minor has created an account, please contact us at gluveloapp@gmail.com and we will take immediate action.

16. Your Rights

Regardless of where you live, you have the following rights over your Gluvelo data:

  • Access: Request a copy of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Correction: Request correction of inaccurate data.
  • Deletion: Delete your account and all associated data at any time from the Profile tab. Data is permanently removed within 30 days.
  • Portability: Request an export of your data in a portable format.
  • Opt out of de-identified data use: You may request that your data not be included in de-identified aggregate data sets used for research or licensing purposes. Email us to make this request.
  • Withdrawal of consent: Stop using the app at any time. Deletion of your account withdraws your consent to our processing of your data.

To exercise any of these rights, email gluveloapp@gmail.com. We will respond within 30 days (or the shorter timeline required by your jurisdiction's law).

17. California Residents — CCPA Rights

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA), as amended by the CPRA, gives you the following additional rights:

  • Know: The right to know what personal information we collect, use, and disclose.
  • Delete: The right to request deletion of your personal information.
  • Correct: The right to request correction of inaccurate personal information.
  • Opt out of sale/sharing: We do not sell or share your personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising. De-identified data that cannot be linked to you is not considered "personal information" under CCPA.
  • Limit use of sensitive personal information: You may request that we limit use of your sensitive personal information (including health data) to only what is necessary to provide the app. Contact us to make this request.
  • Opt out of de-identified data licensing: You may request that your data be excluded from de-identified aggregate data sets. See Section 16.
  • Non-discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of these rights.
  • Authorized agent: You may designate an authorized agent to submit requests on your behalf. See Section 20.

To exercise California privacy rights, email gluveloapp@gmail.com with the subject line "California Privacy Request." We will verify your identity before processing the request.

Categories of personal information collected: Identifiers (name, email); health and medical information; commercial information (subscription status); usage and device data. We do not sell personal information.

18. Washington State Residents — My Health My Data Act

If you are a Washington State resident, the Washington My Health My Data Act (MHMDA), effective 2024, provides additional rights specific to consumer health data. Gluvelo collects data types covered by this law, including medication logs, body measurements, health conditions, and symptom tracking.

Under the MHMDA, you have the right to:

  • Confirm whether we collect your consumer health data and obtain a list of the categories we collect
  • Access the specific consumer health data we have collected about you
  • Withdraw your consent to our collection of consumer health data at any time (by deleting your account)
  • Have your consumer health data deleted
  • Opt out of any sale of consumer health data (we do not sell identifiable consumer health data)
  • Appeal a denial of any of these rights

We do not sell your identifiable consumer health data, share it with third parties for advertising, or use it to track your location. De-identified aggregate data that cannot be linked to an individual is not consumer health data under the MHMDA. To exercise these rights, email gluveloapp@gmail.com with the subject line "Washington Health Data Request."

19. International Users & GDPR

Gluvelo is currently operated from the United States. If you access the app from outside the United States, your data will be transferred to and processed in the U.S., where privacy laws may differ from those in your country.

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), United Kingdom, or Switzerland, the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) or equivalent laws apply. Your rights under GDPR include the right to access, correct, erase, restrict, and port your data, and to object to processing. Our lawful basis for processing your health data is your explicit consent, given when you create an account and enter your health profile.

To exercise GDPR rights or to lodge a complaint, email gluveloapp@gmail.com. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with your local supervisory authority.

20. Authorized Agents

You may designate an authorized agent — such as a family member, caretaker, or attorney — to submit privacy requests on your behalf. To do so, the agent must email gluveloapp@gmail.com with:

  • Your name and the email address associated with your Gluvelo account
  • A written statement signed by you authorizing the agent to act on your behalf
  • The agent's name and contact information

We will verify the authorization before processing any request. This is especially relevant for users in Caretaker Mode who may manage health data on behalf of another person.

21. Cookies & Tracking Technologies

The Gluvelo mobile app uses the following tracking and analytics tools:

  • Firebase Analytics — collects anonymized in-app usage events (screens visited, features tapped). You can opt out of Firebase Analytics data collection by contacting us.
  • Firebase Crashlytics — collects crash reports including device information, OS version, and stack traces. No health data is included.
  • Behavioral Analytics (e.g. Mixpanel or Amplitude) — tracks anonymized in-app behavior to help us understand how features are used. No names, emails, or health data fields are transmitted.

None of these tools receive your identifiable health data. If you visit the Gluvelo website, it may be hosted on Vercel, which logs standard request metadata (IP address, browser type, page visited). We do not use advertising cookies or ad tracking networks on the website or in the app.
